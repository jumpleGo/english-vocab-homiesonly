## Idioms (from books, lessons, real conversations)

| Phrase | Meaning (RU) | Example |
|---|---|---|
| be an eyesore | мозолить глаза, портить вид | Some say that Lakhta centre is an eyesore in the historical centre. |
| what are you driving at? | к чему ты клонишь? | I don't see what you're driving at. |
| color me intrigued/surprised/shocked | считай меня (заинтригованным…) | We're getting a raise this year? Whoa, color me shocked! |
| don't have a cow | не переживай так, не бесись | My mother had a cow when I said I'd made a tattoo. |
| have a beef with someone | враждовать, иметь претензии к кому-то | He has a beef with his neighbor. |
| blanketed in (snow/fog) | укрытый (= covered with) | The mountains are blanketed in snow. |
| umpteenth | сотый/тысячный по счёту | I drank my umpteenth cup of coffee today. |
| know like the back of my hand | знать как свои пять пальцев | I know her like the back of my hand. |
| have a fit | взбеситься, выйти из себя | Mum would have a fit if she saw me now. |
| as if! | вот ещё! ага, конечно! | "You'll do the dishes?" "As if!" |
| in hindsight / with hindsight | задним числом, оглядываясь назад | In hindsight, I've got to admit he was talented. |
| how about that | надо же, как тебе такое | He won the lottery twice — how about that. |
| dodge a bullet | пронесло, избежать неприятности | We dodged a bullet by leaving early. |
| sugar-coat | приукрашивать (неприятную правду) | She never sugar-coated anything. |
| chick lit | лёгкий женский роман (жанр) | I love reading chick lit on vacation. |
